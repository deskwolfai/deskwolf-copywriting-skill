## The opportunity

Most construction firms in the region are sitting on the same AI licenses you are. Copilot in the Microsoft tenancy. ChatGPT seats handed out to whoever asked. A few PMs experimenting with Claude on the side.

Almost none of them have a workforce that actually uses those tools on the real work. The licenses are a sunk cost. The behavior change never happens.

Fullmer can be the first construction firm in [REGION] where every PM, estimator, and admin runs a named AI process inside their week. That position shows up in three places at once: in how fast bids go out, in how clean RFIs and change-order narratives read on the client side, and in how the firm gets talked about when senior estimators and PMs are deciding where to work next.

### What the math looks like

One estimator. Loaded rate $80 an hour. One hour a day recovered on takeoff narratives, proposal copy, and scope-sheet cleanup. That's $20,800 a year, per estimator. Across ten estimators it's $208,000. PMs and admin staff stack on top of that.

That math holds before you count the bids you win because the response went out 48 hours earlier than the competing firm's.

### Why "give them access" hasn't been enough

Access is the easy half. Fluency is the half that compounds. Without a structured one-hour reset, most of the licenses sit unused and the seats that get used are running personal errands more than billable work. We see this pattern in every firm we walk into.

The workshop changes that in one sitting.

## What happens in the hour

In-person at the Fullmer office. Vic runs the room. Every attendee brings their laptop and one real task from their current week.

### Agenda (60 minutes)

**0:00 – 0:10. Where AI actually wins inside a construction firm.**
Specific role examples. Estimators: takeoff narratives, proposal copy, scope-sheet cleanup. PMs: first-draft RFIs, status emails, change-order writeups, submittal log triage. Ops and admin: safety briefs, toolbox talk summaries, training rollups. No theory. Named tasks.

**0:10 – 0:25. Hands-on, in the tool, on a real task.**
Each attendee opens Copilot, ChatGPT, or Claude on their device and walks through one task from their week with Vic and the room. We surface where the tool helps, where it fails, and where the prompt has to change.

**0:25 – 0:45. Use-case build.**
Each attendee scopes one named AI use case they'll run for the next two weeks. We hand out a one-page template: the task, the tool, the prompt pattern, the time baseline, the target.

**0:45 – 0:55. Round the room.**
Each person reads their use case. The group sharpens it. Vic flags the two or three with the biggest payoff so Paige can see them after.

**0:55 – 1:00. Next step.**
A 30-minute call between Vic and Paige to look at what the room scoped and decide where AI fluency goes company-wide.

### What every employee walks out with

One named, scoped AI use case for their role. Written down. Tool picked. Baseline measured. Two-week target. Something like:

> "Drafting first-pass RFI responses in Copilot. Target: cut response drafting time from 25 minutes to 8 minutes. Measured for two weeks against my last ten RFIs."

A working process they own.
