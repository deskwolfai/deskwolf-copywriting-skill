## Strategy Frame

**Output type.** Single-offer landing page acting as the brand homepage. One offer, one CTA destination (call or text the same phone number), no nav. Per the funnel decision matrix: LANDING playbook, stripped-down variant (no VSL, no testimonials yet, no bonus stack — the business does not have the assets to fake them honestly).

**Traffic temperature.** Mixed cold and warm. Most arrivals will be from the source flyer (warm: they saw the flyer, scanned a QR, and want to confirm the service is real), with cold organic search a secondary source (e.g. "cargo van pickup near me," "ikea delivery riverside"). Copy leads with a clear utility hook so cold readers can self-qualify in under five seconds, and credentials follow.

**Value ladder rung.** Frontend, $60+ per job. Tone: low-commitment, impulse-friendly, proof-light. Medium length. The reader should be able to make a "yes, text the number" decision inside one scroll.

**Big Domino.** *"A single-driver cargo van is the right tool for the things one person can lift their side of."* If the reader buys this belief, the alternatives (renting a truck, asking a friend with a pickup, hiring a full crew) feel oversized for the job. Every section reinforces that single belief without naming the competition.

**Value Equation push.** Two of four levers, hard:
- *Time delay* — same-day pickups, "text. quote. done.", "if it fits in the van, it goes today."
- *Effort and sacrifice* — flat quote by text, no rental counter, no mileage charge, no favor-calling. The customer hands over a photo and an address; the van handles the rest.

Dream outcome and likelihood are touched lightly (the FAQ provides proof and reversal). They are not the lead.

**Status check (FBA).** The reader feels in control: handled the heavy thing without bothering anyone, did not pay U-Haul mileage plus gas plus return, got the Marketplace find home before the seller relisted. Status: practical, decisive, knows a guy.

## Anti-Slop Pass

- No em dashes, no double-hyphens.
- No antithetical-contrast constructions ("it's not X, it's Y" / "you don't X, you Y"). Spot-checked; all claims state the positive directly. Hard-cap negatives like "No surprise mileage" are flat lists of absent costs, not contrast constructions.
- No restricted words above the cap (zero "amazing," "seamless," "leverage," "solution," "transform," "innovative," "premier," "world-class").
- Contractions used naturally ("we'd," "you're," "won't," "you'll").
- Sentence length variety: short declaratives ("Text. Quote. Done.") next to longer specifics ("Most pickups go from text to completed haul inside the same day.").
- Specific numbers over vague claims: $60 starting price, 3- or 4-person crew threshold, 26-foot truck threshold, IE / LA / OC named explicitly.
- All claims sourced to the operator's actual service constraints (sourced from the flyer plus reasonable inference). No invented testimonials.
- Voice — direct, confident, technical, partnership, ambitious-at-correct-altitude. Reads like the driver talking across a counter.
- One reader, not a crowd. "You" = singular. No "business owners everywhere" or "folks."

### FAQ (objection handling)
- Eyebrow: `COMMON QUESTIONS`
- H2: **Before you tap call.**
- Q1: How much does a pickup cost? — Flat per job, quoted by text before the van leaves. Small in-town pickups typically start around $60. Longer hauls across counties and multi-stop jobs go up from there. The number on your text is the number you pay.
- Q2: Will you help carry it up or down stairs? — Yes, for items one person can handle their side of (sofas, dressers, washers, IKEA boxes). For couches that need three or four people, a crew-based mover is the right call. Send a photo and you'll get a straight answer.
- Q3: Can you pick something up from Marketplace, OfferUp, or a store? — Yes. Text the seller's address (or the store and pickup ticket), your drop-off address, and a photo of the item. Same-day pickups are common when the seller has windows open.
- Q4: Are you insured? — Yes. Commercial auto with cargo coverage for items on board. A current certificate of insurance can be sent ahead of pickup if a building or HOA needs one.
- Q5: How big is the van? What fits? — High-roof cargo van. Roughly the contents of a single room, or a couch plus a dresser plus a few boxes. Most appliances and IKEA flat-packs fit easily. Send a photo if you're unsure and you'll get a yes or no before booking.
- Q6: Do you take same-day jobs? — Often, yes. Same-day availability depends on what's already on the calendar. Text early in the day for the best shot at an afternoon window.
- Q7: What about really heavy items, pianos, or full-house moves? — Not the right van for those. Anything that calls for three or four people, a piano dolly, or a full 26-foot truck is better handled by a crew-based mover. Honest answer beats a job done badly.

- Spanish language line is included in the schema (`availableLanguage`). Remove if the operator does not actively take calls in Spanish.
