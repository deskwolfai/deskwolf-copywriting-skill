# Sales Copywriter — Complete Framework Reference

This document consolidates every actionable framework from all five source books. Organized by use case, not by book. When writing copy, find the section that matches your output type and apply.

---

## 1. VALUE & OFFER CONSTRUCTION

### 1A. The Value Equation
```
VALUE = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)
```

**Four levers to pull in copy:**

| Driver | Direction | How to Move It in Copy |
|---|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | INCREASE | Paint the end state vividly. Frame in terms of status gained. Use specific numbers. |
| Perceived Likelihood | INCREASE | Stack proof: testimonials, case studies, demos, guarantees, credentials. |
| Time Delay | DECREASE | Emphasize speed. "Results in 48 hours." "Setup takes 15 minutes." |
| Effort & Sacrifice | DECREASE | Emphasize ease. DFY > DWY > DIY. "We handle everything." |

**Infinite Value Principle:** If denominator approaches zero (instant, effortless), perceived value becomes infinite.

**Perception rule:** It doesn't matter if you ACTUALLY reduce time/effort. It matters if they PERCEIVE you did.

### 1B. Grand Slam Offer Construction (5 Steps)

**Step 1 — Dream Outcome:** What does the prospect actually want? Not your service — the destination. Sell the vacation, not the plane flight.

**Step 2 — List ALL Problems (30-60+):** Everything that could prevent the dream outcome. Four categories:
- Dream Outcome problems: "This won't be worth the money"
- Likelihood problems: "This won't work for ME"
- Effort problems: "This will be too hard / confusing"
- Time problems: "This will take too long"

**Step 3 — Flip Problems to Solutions:** Formula: "How to [reverse the problem]"

**Step 4 — Create Delivery Vehicles:** Six dimensions:
1. Attention level: 1-on-1, small group, or 1-to-many?
2. Effort level: DIY, DWY, or DFY?
3. Medium: in-person, phone, email, text, Zoom, chat?
4. Format: audio, video, or written?
5. Response speed: 24/7, business hours, within 5 min, within 24 hrs?
6. 10x/1/10th test: What if they paid 10x? How would you deliver at 1/10th?

**Step 5 — Trim & Stack:**
- Remove: high cost + low value FIRST, then low cost + low value
- Keep: low cost + high value AND high cost + high value
- Bundle remaining. Give each a benefit-driven name + dollar value.

### 1C. The Value Ladder (Offer Architecture)

Five rungs, bottom to top:

| Rung | Price | Purpose | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bait / Free | $0 | Lead generation, build trust | Lead magnet, free audit, calculator |
| Frontend | $1-$100 | Acquire customers at breakeven | SLO digital product, template kit |
| Middle | $100-$2,000 | Core revenue driver | Monthly service, course, group program |
| Backend | $2,000-$100K | Premium, high-touch | Done-for-you, mastermind, custom build |
| Peak | Custom | Highest level | Equity deals, licensing, franchise |

**Rules:**
- Every business needs a ladder, not just one offer
- Each rung delivers MORE value than the price
- You can't sell high-ticket to someone who hasn't experienced lower tiers
- Backend is where the real money is — frontend covers ad costs
- If revenue is stuck, you're missing a rung

### 1D. Four Offer Types (Money Model Sequence)

**Attraction Offers (strangers -> customers):**
1. Win Your Money Back — pay upfront, hit goal, get refund/credit
2. Giveaways — enter for grand prize, everyone gets discount offer
3. Decoy Offer — free/cheap option next to loaded premium (80% take premium)
4. Buy X Get Y Free — give more free than paid, raise prices first
5. Pay Less Now or Pay More Later — discount + bonuses now vs. free start + full price later

**Upsell Offers (spend more):**
1. Classic — "You can't have X without Y" (solve the next problem)
2. Menu — multiple options, most expensive first
3. Anchor — show very expensive option first, real offer feels reasonable
4. Rollover — apply credits/winnings from one offer toward the next

**Downsell Offers (turn no's into yes's):**
1. Payment Plans — work from most cash upfront to least (7-step waterfall)
2. Trial With Penalty — free if they meet terms, card on file
3. Feature Downsells — lower price by REMOVING features (never discount)

**Continuity Offers (keep buying):**
1. Bonus Offers — awesome free thing IF they sign up for continuity
2. Discount Offers — free time for term commitment (bill every 4 weeks = 8.3% more revenue)
3. Waived Fee — "Month-to-month with setup fee, or waive it for annual"

## 2. OFFER ENHANCERS

### 2A. Scarcity (Limit Quantity)
- **Total Business Cap:** "We only accept 25 clients total"
- **Growth Rate Cap:** "We only accept 5 new clients per week"
- **Cohort Cap:** "We take 100 clients 4x per year"
- Rule: Define REAL capacity. Advertise it. Always sell out. Never fake it.

### 2B. Urgency (Limit Time)
- **Cohort Rolling:** "Sign up today, start Monday. Otherwise wait until next month."
- **Rolling Seasonal:** Rename same promotion by season with real dates
- **Pricing/Bonus-Based:** "This bonus only available during this window"
- **Exploding Opportunity:** The opportunity itself decays with time

### 2C. Bonuses (Increase Value)
1. Always offer them — present each individually
2. Give each a benefit-driven name
3. Explain: how it relates to their problem, what it is, how you created it
4. Provide proof (stat, client result, personal experience)
5. Assign a price tag and justify it
6. Tools, checklists, templates > additional trainings
7. Each bonus should address a specific objection
8. Bonus value should ECLIPSE the core offer value
9. Add scarcity/urgency to bonuses themselves
- **Never discount your core offer. ADD bonuses instead.**

### 2D. Guarantees (Reverse Risk)
Formula: "If you do not get [X result] in [Y time], we will [Z]."

| Type | What It Is | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Unconditional | No questions asked refund | High-trust, proven product |
| Conditional | Must meet terms to qualify. Can be "better than money back" | Most versatile. Rewards effort. |
| Anti-Guarantee | "All sales are final." Lean into it with a reason. | Premium/exclusive positioning |
| Implied | Performance-based (revshare, pay-per-result) | Services, agencies |

**Guarantee math:** If guarantee increases sales 30% but doubles refunds from 5% to 10%: Old = 95 net. New = 117 net. 23% more revenue.

### 2E. M.A.G.I.C. Naming Formula
- **M** — Magnetic Reason Why (Free, 88% Off, Grand Opening, Limited)
- **A** — Avatar (San Bernardino Contractors, Busy Restaurant Owners)
- **G** — Goal / Dream Outcome (Never Miss A Call, Double Your Bookings)
- **I** — Interval (7-Day, 21-Day, 90-Day)
- **C** — Container (Challenge, Blueprint, Bootcamp, System, Launchpad)

**When offers fatigue, refresh in this order:**
1. Change the creative
2. Change the body copy
3. Change the headline / offer name
4. Change the duration
5. Change the enhancer
6. Change the monetization structure (last resort)

## 3. PERSUASION & STORYTELLING

### 3A. The Epiphany Bridge

The core story structure. Use for VSLs, webinar content, email stories, landing page narratives.

1. **The Backstory:** Where you were (same place as prospect)
2. **The Journey:** What you tried, what failed
3. **The Epiphany:** The single moment/realization that changed everything
4. **The Framework:** The system you built from that epiphany
5. **The Achievement:** The result you got
6. **The Transformation:** Who you became

**Rules:**
- One epiphany per story — don't stack multiple insights
- The audience must FEEL the emotion, not just hear the logic
- Speak to the identity they WANT, not the one they have
- Use "false start" stories — two failed attempts before the breakthrough

### 3B. The Big Domino

"If [prospect] believes that [New Opportunity] is the key to [desire] AND is only achievable through [your framework], then all other objections become irrelevant."

- Your entire piece should be designed to knock over this one domino
- Every story, proof point, and demo serves this single belief
- If the Big Domino falls, the sale is made
- Define the Big Domino BEFORE writing anything

### 3C. False Belief Patterns (3 Objection Categories)

Every prospect has three categories of false beliefs:

| Category | The Belief | Your Job |
|---|---|---|
| Vehicle | "This particular opportunity won't work" | Prove the New Opportunity is the right vehicle |
| Internal | "I can't do this — I'm not smart/skilled enough" | Show people like them succeed |
| External | "External factors will prevent this — economy, competition, timing" | Neutralize every external excuse |

Each false belief needs its own Epiphany Bridge story to break it.

### 3D. New Opportunity Framing

**NEVER** position as an improvement to what they're doing:
- Improvement = "You failed before, try harder this time" = high resistance
- New Opportunity = "Forget all that, here's a completely new vehicle" = high excitement

**Opportunity Switch:** Replace their current approach entirely
- "Stop chasing leads. Let calls come to you."

**Opportunity Stack:** Add something new on top of what they do
- "Keep your current marketing. Add an AI receptionist that catches what you miss."

### 3E. The Attractive Character (Personal Brand Voice)

**Four Elements:**
1. **Backstory:** Origin story connecting to audience's current pain
2. **Parables:** Teaching stories used repeatedly (your "greatest hits")
3. **Character Flaws:** Deliberate imperfections that make you relatable
4. **Polarity:** Strong stance that repels some to attract others harder

**Four Identity Types (pick primary):**
1. **The Leader:** "Follow me, I've been where you are"
2. **The Adventurer:** "I went on this journey and brought back the treasure"
3. **The Reporter:** "I found the experts and compiled the answers"
4. **The Reluctant Hero:** "I didn't want to share this, but I feel obligated"

**Six Core Storylines (rotate in content):**
1. Loss & Redemption — had it all, lost it, clawed back
2. Us vs. Them — our movement vs. the establishment
3. Before & After — transformation with vivid contrast
4. Amazing Discovery — stumbled onto something game-changing
5. Secret Telling — sharing what nobody else will
6. Third Person Proof — customer success through their eyes

## 4. PRESENTATIONS & SCRIPTS

### 4A. The Perfect Webinar Script

Works for: webinars, VSLs, stage presentations, sales calls, long-form sales pages.

**Introduction (First 5 Minutes):**
1. State the Big Promise (one clear outcome)
2. Quick credibility stack (not a resume — specific results)
3. Future Pace: "By the end of this, you'll know exactly how to [result]"
4. Pre-frame the offer: "I'll show you the framework, and at the end, if you want help implementing, I'll show you how"

**The 3 Secrets (Core Content):**

Secret 1 — Destroys Vehicle false belief:
- Epiphany Bridge story about discovering the vehicle
- Teach the framework/concept
- Show proof it works

Secret 2 — Destroys Internal false belief:
- Epiphany Bridge story about overcoming self-doubt
- Show regular people succeeding
- Reframe their limitation as an advantage

Secret 3 — Destroys External false belief:
- Epiphany Bridge story about overcoming external obstacles
- Neutralize the #1 external excuse
- Show external factors don't determine outcomes

**The Stack & Close:**
1. Transition: "Let me show you everything you're getting"
2. The Stack Slide: List every component with dollar values
3. Trial Closes: "If all this did was [smallest benefit], would it be worth [price]?"
4. Price Reveal: Total value -> actual price (massive gap)
5. Urgency/Scarcity: Why act now
6. Guarantee: Risk reversal
7. Final CTA: Clear, single action

### 4B. The Stack (Value Presentation)

**Formula:**
1. Main framework / service / product
2. Tool #1 that accelerates results
3. Tool #2 that removes a specific obstacle
4. Tangible bonus (templates, swipe files, scripts)
5. Access bonus (community, coaching, support)
6. Speed bonus (fast-start guide, done-for-you setup)

**Rules:**
- Dollar value on each component
- Total stack value = 10x+ the asking price
- Present each item individually — never lump together
- The Stack Slide is the most important visual in any presentation

### 4C. Trial Close Framework

Between each major section, ask:
- "If all this did was [benefit A], would it be worth [price]?"
- "If all this did was [benefit B], would it be worth [price]?"
- By the time you reach the close, they've said yes 3+ times

## 5. ADVERTISING & LEAD GENERATION

### 5A. The Core Four (Only 4 Ways to Advertise)

| Method | Type | Key Principle |
|---|---|---|
| Warm Outreach | 1-to-1, people who know you | 100/day, ACA framework, start free |
| Free Content | 1-to-many, people who know you | Volume works. 10x output = 10x growth. |
| Cold Outreach | 1-to-1, strangers | Personalize + big fast value in <30 sec |
| Paid Ads | 1-to-many, strangers | Call Out + Value + CTA |

### 5B. Call Out Methods (Ad Headlines)

- **Labels:** "[City] [Industry] Owners" — local + specific = best
- **Yes-Questions:** "Do you miss calls after 5pm?" — instant self-identification
- **If-Then:** "If you're losing 5+ calls a week, we can fix that in 48 hours"
- **Ridiculous Results:** "This plumber added $4,200/month without a single new ad"
- **Non-verbal:** Contrast, likeness, scene-setting in visuals

### 5C. What-Who-When Matrix (Ad Angle Generator)

**WHAT (8 angles):** Four value drivers + four opposites
1. Dream Outcome achieved
2. Dream Outcome NOT achieved (pain of missing out)
3. High likelihood of success
4. Low likelihood without this
5. Speed / fast results
6. Slowness / delay without this
7. Ease / effortlessness
8. Difficulty / pain of doing it yourself

**WHO (6 perspectives):** How does each person in their life see them?
- Spouse, Kids, Friends, Boss/Colleagues, Rivals, Competitors

**WHEN (3 timelines):**
- Past ("Remember when you..."), Present ("Right now you're..."), Future ("Imagine when...")

**= 144 unique angles per product.** Generate variants, test winners, kill losers.

### 5D. Lead Magnet Framework (7 Steps)

1. Pick the problem (narrow, specific, solvable)
2. Pick the method: reveal their problem, sample/trial, or one step of multi-step
3. Pick the delivery: Software, Information, Services, or Physical
4. Test the name (headline > image > subheadline)
5. Make it easy to consume (multiple formats)
6. Make it darn good — free stuff IS your reputation
7. Clear CTA with scarcity/urgency to learn more

**Principle:** "Give away the secrets, sell the implementation."

### 5E. The Rule of 100

100 primary actions per day for 100 days:
- Warm/Cold Outreach: 100 reach outs per day
- Content: 100 minutes per day creating
- Paid Ads: 100 minutes per day + $X/day budget for 100 days

### 5F. Dream 100 (Audience Borrowing)

1. List 100 targets: podcasts, groups, influencers, email lists, YouTube channels
2. Build relationships BEFORE you need them
3. Work your way in: guest appearances, collabs, JV offers, affiliate deals
4. Goal: Get in front of their audience with YOUR Epiphany Bridge story

## 6. EMAIL SEQUENCES

### 6A. Soap Opera Sequence (New Subscribers, Days 1-5)

| Day | Purpose | Structure |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Set the stage | "Something amazing happened and I need to tell you about it" + open loop |
| 2 | High drama / backstory | The conflict and tension + open loop |
| 3 | Epiphany | The breakthrough moment + open loop |
| 4 | Hidden benefits | What else changed that they didn't expect + open loop |
| 5 | Urgency + CTA | Why they need to act now |

**Key:** Every email ends with a cliffhanger that makes them open the next.

### 6B. Seinfeld Emails (Ongoing After Soap Opera)

- Daily emails, no fixed structure
- Entertaining FIRST, selling second
- Each email tells a story from life and ties it to a lesson/offer
- Subject line is everything — curiosity-driven, never descriptive
- Be interesting. Boring emails get unsubscribes. Selling emails get sales.

### 6C. ACA Framework (Warm Outreach Replies)

When they respond to outreach:
1. **Acknowledge** what they said
2. **Compliment** something specific about them
3. **Ask** another question to keep the conversation going

After 3-4 exchanges, make an offer using the Value Equation.

### 6D. Cold Outreach Email Principles

- Research 1-3 things about each prospect
- Blow their minds in under 30 seconds
- Give away stuff people actually pay for
- Follow up more times in more ways (email -> call -> DM -> voicemail)
- Restart the list every 3-6 months

## 7. FUNNEL ARCHITECTURE

### 7A. Traffic Temperature -> Funnel Matching

| Temperature | Entry Point | Pre-Frame Needed? |
|---|---|---|
| Hot | Direct to offer page | No |
| Warm | Bridge content (case study, video, blog) -> offer | Yes, light |
| Cold | Hook -> Story -> Bridge -> Offer | Yes, full journey |

**Never send cold traffic directly to an offer page.**

### 7B. The Seven Core Funnels

| Funnel | Purpose | Structure | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free + Shipping | Acquire customers at breakeven | Opt-in -> Free offer + shipping -> Order bump -> Upsell | Books, samples |
| SLO | Acquire + profit | Squeeze -> $27-97 offer -> Upsell -> Downsell | Digital products |
| Continuity | Recurring revenue | Sales page -> Trial/discount first month -> Member area | Software, communities |
| Perfect Webinar | Core revenue | Registration -> Webinar -> Offer -> Checkout | $297-$2K programs |
| Invisible | Try before you buy | Register free -> Consume -> Pay if satisfied | High-trust products |
| Product Launch | Build anticipation | 4 pre-launch videos over 7-10 days -> Cart open -> Cart close | New launches |
| High-Ticket Application | Premium clients | VSL -> Application -> Phone call -> Close | $3K+ services |

### 7C. Funnel Stacking (Complete Business Model)

Chain funnels together as Value Ladder rungs:
1. Dream 100 -> audience access
2. Free + Shipping book funnel -> acquires customers at breakeven
3. SLO funnel -> profits from low-ticket, funds ads
4. Webinar funnel -> sells core program
5. Application funnel -> sells high-ticket mastermind/service
6. Continuity -> recurring revenue underneath everything

Each funnel is an entry point to the next.

### 7D. Seven Phases of a Funnel

1. Determine traffic temperature
2. Set up the pre-frame bridge
3. Qualify subscribers (lead magnet filters for intent)
4. Qualify buyers (low-ticket offer separates browsers from buyers)
5. Identify hyperactive buyers (immediate upsells catch impulse buyers)
6. Age and ascend (nurture, move up Value Ladder)
7. Change selling environment (higher ticket = higher touch: phone, webinar, live)

## 8. PRICING & REVENUE OPTIMIZATION

### 8A. Premium Pricing Psychology

**Niche pricing multiplier (same content, 100x price via specificity):**
- Generic "Time Management" course: $19
- "Time Management for Sales Professionals": $99
- "Time Management for B2B Outbound Sales Reps": $499
- "Time Management for B2B Outbound Power Tools & Gardening Sales Reps": $2,000

**Virtuous cycle of raising prices:**
Increase emotional investment -> Increase perceived value -> Increase results -> Attract best clients -> Multiply margin -> Raise prices again

**Never discount. Add bonuses instead.**

### 8B. Payment Plan Waterfall (Downsell Sequence)

Work from most cash upfront to least:
1. Full price with discount for paying in full
2. Third-party financing / credit card / layaway
3. Split into 2 payments aligned with paycheck dates
4. Confirm desire (1-10 scale, need 8+)
5. Split into 3 payments
6. Spread payments across service duration
7. Trial With Penalty (last resort)

**Churn data by billing frequency:**
- Monthly: 10.7% churn
- Quarterly: 5% churn
- Annual: 2% churn
- Start with highest commitment always.

### 8C. Client Financed Acquisition

Cover the cost to GET and FULFILL a customer within 30 days. Once you can do this, money is no longer the bottleneck — recycle cash into more acquisition forever.

**Target metric: LTGP:CAC ratio of 3:1 or better.**

### 8D. Continuity Revenue Tactics

- Bill every 4 weeks instead of monthly (13 cycles vs 12 = 8.3% more revenue, 41% more profit on 20% margins)
- Use titles (Silver, Gold, Diamond) for retention — status > discounts
- Get two forms of payment (waive processing fee for second)
- Offer lifetime discount at highest-churn month
- Always do exit interviews — save ~33% of customers who agree
- Gift cards as retention + referral lead magnet

### 8E. Supply & Demand

Keep supply below demand. Maximize profits and desire.

**Fractal Demand Model:** 1/5 of prospects will pay 5x the price. Selling 2 at $5,000 ($10K) > selling 10 at $500 ($5K).

**Hormozi Law:** The longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make.
