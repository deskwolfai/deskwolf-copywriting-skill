# Sales Copywriter — Output Templates

Structural templates for every output type. Follow the skeleton, fill with frameworks from `frameworks.md`, and apply anti-slop rules from `../copywriting_guidelines.md`.

---

## 1. LANDING PAGE / HERO SECTION

**Frameworks:** Value Equation + Call Out Methods + Big Domino

```
[CALL OUT] — Label, Yes-Question, If-Then, or Ridiculous Result

[HEADLINE] — Dream Outcome + Specific Audience
[SUBHEADLINE] — Push Perceived Likelihood or reduce Time Delay

[HERO COPY — 2-3 sentences max]
- State the problem in their language
- Introduce the New Opportunity (not improvement)
- Specific proof point or result

[PRIMARY CTA] — One clear action, benefit-framed
("Book Your 15-Min Discovery Call" not "Contact Us")

[SOCIAL PROOF BAR] — Logos, "X businesses served", specific stat
```

**Rules:**
- Above the fold: headline + subheadline + CTA + one proof element
- No jargon. Write at 6th grade reading level.
- Big Domino belief should be implied in the headline

## 2. OFFER STACK PAGE

**Frameworks:** Grand Slam Offer 5-Step + The Stack + M.A.G.I.C. Naming

```
[OFFER NAME] — M.A.G.I.C. formula
(Magnetic Reason + Avatar + Goal + Interval + Container)

[THE PROBLEM SECTION]
- Name 3-5 specific problems they face (from the 30-60 problem list)
- Use their exact language, not industry terms

[THE NEW OPPORTUNITY]
- This is NOT an improvement. This is a new vehicle.
- Epiphany Bridge micro-story (3-4 sentences)

[THE STACK]
Component 1: [Name] — [What it does for them] .......... Value: $X
Component 2: [Name] — [What it does for them] .......... Value: $X
Component 3: [Name] — [What it does for them] .......... Value: $X
Bonus 1: [Name] — [What it does for them] .............. Value: $X
Bonus 2: [Name] — [What it does for them] .............. Value: $X
Bonus 3: [Name] — [What it does for them] .............. Value: $X
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Total Value: $XX,XXX
Today's Price: $X,XXX

[GUARANTEE]
"If you do not get [X result] in [Y time], we will [Z]."
Choose type: unconditional, conditional, anti, or implied.

[URGENCY / SCARCITY]
- Honest capacity limit, cohort deadline, or bonus expiration

[FINAL CTA]
One button. Benefit-framed.

[FAQ SECTION]
- Address remaining Vehicle, Internal, and External false beliefs
```

## 3. PRICING PAGE

**Frameworks:** Decoy Offer + Value Ladder + Anchor Upsell

```
[SECTION HEADLINE] — "Choose the right fit for your business"

[TIER STRUCTURE — 3 options, middle is the target]

TIER 1 (Decoy / Stripped):        TIER 2 (Target / Loaded):        TIER 3 (Anchor / Premium):
- Fewer features                  - Full feature set                - Everything in Tier 2
- No guarantee                    - Conditional guarantee           - Plus: 1-on-1, DFY, priority
- Basic delivery                  - Bonuses included                - Anti-guarantee or implied
- Self-serve only                 - Support included                - White-glove everything
$X/mo                            $XX/mo ← MOST POPULAR             $XXX/mo

[COMPARISON TABLE]
Feature-by-feature breakdown. Check marks and X marks.
Tier 2 should have the best value-to-price ratio.

[SOCIAL PROOF]
Specific testimonial per tier if possible.

[FAQ]
Payment plans, refund policy, what happens after signup.
```

**Rules:**
- Anchor high first (Tier 3 makes Tier 2 feel like a deal)
- 80% should choose Tier 2 when contrast is large enough
- "Most Popular" badge on target tier
- Never discount. Add bonuses to make the target tier irresistible.

## 4. COLD EMAIL / DM SEQUENCE

**Frameworks:** Core Four Cold Outreach + ACA + Big Fast Value

```
EMAIL 1 — THE OPENER (Day 1)
Subject: [Personalized — reference something specific about them]
Body:
- 1 sentence: what you noticed about their business (specific)
- 1 sentence: the result you can create (Value Equation — dream outcome + speed)
- 1 sentence: proof (one specific case study or stat)
- CTA: Low-commitment ask ("Worth a quick look?" or "Can I send you [free thing]?")

EMAIL 2 — THE VALUE DROP (Day 3)
Subject: [Curiosity-driven, no pitch in subject line]
Body:
- Lead with something useful — a tip, insight, or quick audit of their business
- Give away stuff people pay for (big fast value in <30 seconds)
- Soft CTA: "Happy to walk you through this if helpful"

EMAIL 3 — THE PROOF (Day 5)
Subject: [Reference a result — "How [similar business] did X"]
Body:
- Third Person Proof storyline (customer success through their eyes)
- Specific numbers: revenue gained, time saved, calls handled
- CTA: Slightly more direct ("Want me to show you what this looks like for [their business]?")

EMAIL 4 — THE BREAK-UP (Day 8)
Subject: "Not a fit?"
Body:
- Acknowledge they're busy (not guilt — respect)
- Restate the core value one more time (one sentence)
- "If timing isn't right, no worries. But if [pain point] is still an issue, I'm here."
- Leave the door open
```

**Rules:**
- Research 1-3 things about each prospect before writing
- Never open with "I" — open with them
- Each email under 100 words
- No attachments in cold emails
- Follow up more times in more ways (email -> LinkedIn -> voicemail)

## 5. WELCOME EMAIL SERIES (SOAP OPERA SEQUENCE)

**Frameworks:** Soap Opera Sequence + Epiphany Bridge + Open Loops

```
EMAIL 1 — SET THE STAGE (Immediately after opt-in)
Subject: [Curiosity — "This changed everything for me"]
Body:
- Thank them for joining
- Tease: "Something happened recently that I need to tell you about..."
- Brief context — don't reveal the punchline
- Open loop: "I'll tell you the whole story tomorrow. But first..."
- Deliver whatever they opted in for (lead magnet link)

EMAIL 2 — HIGH DRAMA / BACKSTORY (Day 2)
Subject: [Cliffhanger from Email 1]
Body:
- Epiphany Bridge: Backstory + Journey (the struggle, what failed)
- Build tension — let them feel the frustration you felt
- Open loop: "And then I discovered something that changed everything. I'll share it tomorrow."

EMAIL 3 — THE EPIPHANY (Day 3)
Subject: [Payoff from Email 2's cliffhanger]
Body:
- The breakthrough moment — the single realization
- What you built from it (the framework)
- Show the result
- Open loop: "But that wasn't even the best part..."

EMAIL 4 — HIDDEN BENEFITS (Day 4)
Subject: [Reference the unexpected upside]
Body:
- What ELSE changed that you didn't expect
- Social proof from others who followed the same path
- Connect to their situation: "If you're dealing with [pain], this is exactly what I mean"
- Open loop: "Tomorrow I'm going to make you an offer. But it's not what you think."

EMAIL 5 — URGENCY + CTA (Day 5)
Subject: [Direct — time to act]
Body:
- Recap the transformation arc briefly (1-2 sentences)
- Present the offer — Value Equation framing
- Urgency/scarcity (real, not fake)
- Clear single CTA
- Guarantee
```

## 6. ONGOING NEWSLETTER / BROADCAST (SEINFELD EMAILS)

**Frameworks:** Seinfeld Emails + Attractive Character Storylines

```
SUBJECT LINE: Curiosity-driven, never descriptive
- Bad: "March Newsletter Update"
- Good: "The $4,200 phone call nobody answered"
- Good: "I watched a restaurant lose 11 customers in one hour"

BODY STRUCTURE:
[STORY — 60% of email]
- Something that happened to you, a client, or someone you observed
- Vivid, specific, sensory details
- Keep it interesting — if you're bored writing it, they're bored reading it

[LESSON — 20% of email]
- One clear takeaway tied to the story
- Connect to their situation

[BRIDGE TO OFFER — 20% of email]
- Natural transition, not a hard pivot
- "If you want help with exactly this, here's what we do..."
- Soft CTA or link

STORYLINE ROTATION (cycle through these):
1. Loss & Redemption
2. Us vs. Them
3. Before & After
4. Amazing Discovery
5. Secret Telling
6. Third Person Proof (customer story)
```

**Rules:**
- Be interesting FIRST, sell second
- Subject line is everything
- One story, one lesson, one CTA per email
- Daily or 3x/week minimum — volume builds relationship
- Never start with "Hey [Name], hope you're doing well"

## 7. AD COPY (META / GOOGLE / SOCIAL)

**Frameworks:** What-Who-When Matrix + Call Out Methods + Traffic Temperature

```
STRUCTURE:
[CALL OUT] — Stop the scroll. Identify the reader.
[HOOK] — One sentence that creates tension or curiosity
[BODY] — 2-4 sentences: problem, agitation, solution preview
[PROOF] — One specific result (number, name, timeframe)
[CTA] — One clear action with benefit

CALL OUT VARIANTS (generate 3-5 per ad):
- Label: "[City] [Industry] owners"
- Yes-Question: "Still missing calls after 5pm?"
- If-Then: "If you lose 5+ calls a week, read this"
- Ridiculous Result: "This dentist added $8K/month. Changed nothing except one thing."

AD ANGLE GENERATION (What-Who-When):
Pick one from each:
- WHAT: [value driver or its opposite]
- WHO: [perspective — how does spouse/friend/competitor see them?]
- WHEN: [past/present/future timeline]

Example angles:
- Dream outcome + competitor perspective + future = "In 6 months, your competitors will wonder what changed"
- Pain of delay + self perspective + present = "Every call you miss right now is revenue walking to someone else"
- Ease + spouse perspective + future = "Imagine telling your partner the business runs itself after 6pm"
```

**Platform notes:**
- Meta: image/video + short copy. Hook in first line (truncation). No links in body on Threads/X.
- Google: Headline 1 (30 chars) + Headline 2 (30 chars) + Description (90 chars). Front-load keywords.
- LinkedIn: Professional tone, longer copy OK, lead with insight not pitch.

## 8. LEAD MAGNET OPT-IN PAGE

**Frameworks:** 7-Step Lead Magnet + Pre-Frame Bridge + Value Equation

```
[HEADLINE] — Specific result + specific audience
"The Exact [Framework/Calculator/Checklist] [Avatar] Use to [Dream Outcome]"

[SUBHEADLINE] — Reduce perceived effort/time
"Takes 2 minutes. No signup required. Instant access."

[3-4 BULLET POINTS] — What they'll learn/get
- Each bullet is a mini dream outcome
- Use "How to..." or "The [number] [thing] that..." format
- Be specific: numbers, names, timeframes

[SOCIAL PROOF] — One stat or testimonial
"Used by 200+ [avatars] in [timeframe]"

[OPT-IN FORM]
- Minimum fields (email only if possible, or email + first name)
- Button text: benefit-framed ("Get My Free [Thing]" not "Submit")

[PRIVACY LINE]
"We respect your inbox. No spam. Unsubscribe anytime."
```

**Rules:**
- One goal per page: get the email
- Remove navigation — no distracting links
- Lead magnet must solve a NARROW problem completely
- The solved narrow problem should reveal a BIGGER problem your paid offer solves

## 9. VSL / WEBINAR SCRIPT

**Frameworks:** Perfect Webinar + Epiphany Bridge + 3 False Beliefs + The Stack

```
INTRO (5 minutes):
"By the end of this [presentation/video], you'll know exactly how to [BIG PROMISE]."
- Quick credibility stack (specific results, not resume)
- Future pace: paint what life looks like after they learn this
- Pre-frame: "At the end, I'll show you how we can do this together — but only if it makes sense"

SECRET 1 — THE NEW OPPORTUNITY (10-15 minutes):
[Destroys Vehicle false belief: "This won't work"]
- Epiphany Bridge story: how you discovered this approach
- Teach the core concept/framework
- Proof: case study, demo, or data
- Trial close: "If all this did was [benefit A], would it be worth [price]?"

SECRET 2 — THE INTERNAL BELIEF BREAK (10-15 minutes):
[Destroys Internal false belief: "I can't do this"]
- Epiphany Bridge story: how you or someone like them overcame self-doubt
- Show regular people succeeding
- Reframe their limitation as an advantage
- Trial close: "If all this did was [benefit B], would it be worth [price]?"

SECRET 3 — THE EXTERNAL BELIEF BREAK (10-15 minutes):
[Destroys External false belief: "It won't work because of X"]
- Epiphany Bridge story: overcoming external obstacles
- Neutralize #1 external excuse (economy, competition, timing, tech complexity)
- Trial close: "If all this did was [benefit C], would it be worth [price]?"

THE STACK & CLOSE (10-15 minutes):
"Let me show you everything you're getting..."
- Present each component individually with dollar values
- Total value vs. actual price (massive gap)
- Guarantee
- Urgency/scarcity
- Final CTA
- "You have two choices: go back to [old way] or [take the new opportunity]"
```

## 11. DOWNSELL PAGE

**Frameworks:** Money Models Downsells (Payment Plans, Feature Strip, Trial)

```
[HEADLINE] — "Before you go — what if we made this easier?"

[ACKNOWLEDGE]
"I understand [original offer] might not be the right fit today."

[THE DOWNSELL — pick one approach:]

Option A — Payment Plan:
"What if you could get started for just $[smaller amount] today?"
- Same product, split payments
- Show per-day cost ("Less than your daily coffee")

Option B — Feature Downsell:
"Here's a lighter version that still gets you [core result]"
- Remove features from highest to lowest value
- Never call it a "discount" — it's a "different option"

Option C — Trial:
"Try it free for [X days]. If you love it, keep it."
- Card on file
- Conditional terms
- Check-in meetings built in

[CTA]
"Get Started for $[amount]"
```

## 12. GUARANTEE SECTION

**Frameworks:** 4 Guarantee Types

```
UNCONDITIONAL:
"Try [product] for [X] days. If you don't [result], email us and we'll refund every penny.
No questions. No forms. No hassle."

CONDITIONAL:
"If you [complete the required steps] and don't [achieve X result] within [Y time],
we'll [refund you / keep working for free / give you Z]."
- Define steps clearly
- Make steps likely to produce results
- "Better than money back" version: refund + bonus for their trouble

ANTI-GUARANTEE:
"All sales are final. We're selective about who we work with because we invest heavily
in every client. If we accept you, we're all in — and we expect you to be too."
- Only use for premium/exclusive positioning
- Must be earned through demonstrated value elsewhere on page

IMPLIED:
"You only pay when [result happens]."
- Performance-based, revshare, pay-per-result
- Strongest form: zero risk for client
```

[Same structure]
Value: $[amount]

"That's $X,XXX in bonuses — yours free when you [action] today."

## 16. FUNNEL SITEMAPS — COMPLETE PAGE SEQUENCES

**Frameworks:** All 15 templates above, chained. Plus Big Domino + Traffic Temperature + Value Ladder positioning.

These two meta-templates show how to chain individual page templates into a complete funnel. Use one when the prompt is "write the full homepage funnel" or "write the full landing page copy" — anything that names the page-as-a-whole rather than a single section.

### When to use

| Page type | Template | Traffic |
|---|---|---|
| Brand-hub homepage | Homepage Funnel below | Mixed (any temperature lands here) |
| Single-offer sales page | Landing Page Funnel below | Cold or warm (ad / affiliate destination) |
| Hybrid (homepage = landing) | Landing Page Funnel + Meet the Founders carve-out | Cold to warm |
| Waitlist / pre-launch wall | Landing Page Funnel (stripped: Hero + opt-in + Footer) | Cold |
| Lead-capture form page | Template #8 (Lead Magnet Opt-In) — not a funnel | Any |

Disambiguation tells (mirrors the frontend-design skill so structure and copy stay aligned):

- Multiple offers OR nav-driven exploration → Homepage Funnel.
- One offer, one CTA destination, no nav → Landing Page Funnel.
- Path is `/industries/<x>` or `/<offer-slug>` → Landing Page Funnel.

### HOMEPAGE FUNNEL (Brand-Hub Pattern)

The homepage opens the relationship. Lower commitment, multi-CTA, brand-storytelling primacy. Visitor leaves with one of three things: an email (newsletter), a download (lead magnet), or a click into the value ladder (primary offer or industry page).

```
[1. HERO] — Template #1 + Big Domino + Value Equation
   Sub-headline: keyword + audience call-out
   Headline: outcome the brand achieves (Big Domino installed here)
   Body: 2-3 sentences expanding the headline into desire
   Primary CTA: action verb + reward ("Book my discovery call")
   Social proof bar: 4-8 logos OR "[N] businesses served" with concrete number

[2. LEAD MAGNET] — Template #8 (compressed, 2 fields max)
   Headline: curiosity gap + specific outcome of the resource
   Subline: what's in it for them, in concrete nouns
   Form: email + first name only
   Button: imperative verb + reward ("Send me the playbook"), never "Submit"

[3. INTRO / VISION] — Attractive Character + Origin sliver
   One paragraph max
   Names who it's for + the wedge / what makes the brand different
   Calls to those at a "reassessment" stage of their journey
   No social proof here — that's section 5's job

[4. PRIMARY OFFER] — Template #2 compressed (no full Stack)
   Offer name (M.A.G.I.C. formula)
   2-3 sentences: dream outcome + new opportunity framing
   CTA: "Learn more" → links to the offer's full page (Landing Page Funnel)
   This section is the single biggest conversion lever on the homepage

[5. CUSTOMER TESTIMONIALS] — Result-Reframe pattern
   3-5 testimonials minimum
   Each: name + role + specific numeric result + quote
   No generic praise. Specificity gates approval.

[6. OTHER OFFERS INDEX] — Value Ladder visualization
   Card grid linking to sub-offers or sub-properties
   Each card: offer name + 1-line outcome (not features)
   Cap at 12 cards. Each links to a Landing Page Funnel destination.

[7. MEET THE TEAM / FOUNDERS] — Attractive Character + Origin Story
   1-4 cards
   Each: founder name + role + 1-line bio + 1 credibility signal
   Bio uses the founder's voice, not corporate prose
   "Vic is the CEO of DeskWolf" → wrong; "Vic builds AI receptionists for local businesses" → right

[8. SOCIAL / BLOG] — Bridge Content (Template #15) micro-version
   Optional. 3 latest posts, card grid.
   Each card: title + excerpt + read time
   Title carries the curiosity gap; excerpt does NOT spoil the article

[9. NEWSLETTER] — Soap Opera (Template #5) lead-in + Seinfeld (Template #6) ongoing
   Headline: concrete value of subscribing ("Weekly AI playbooks for [audience]")
   Never "Stay in touch" or "Join our newsletter"
   Form: email-only field
   Button: imperative + outcome ("Send me the playbooks")

[10. FOOTER] — Utility, no copy framework
   Nav links, legal, social icons. Address only if locally-bound.
   No mid-content pitches, no "one last offer" hidden in the footer.
```

**Voice through-line (Homepage):**
- Multi-CTA tolerated — three different destinations is fine (lead magnet form, primary offer, newsletter).
- Lower commitment ramp throughout — no high-pressure language, no scarcity.
- Brand-storytelling primacy — Intro/Vision and Meet the Team get more space than they would on a landing page.
- Big Domino installed in Hero, reinforced in Primary Offer, never re-stated. Repetition feels desperate on a homepage.
- "We" = the brand. "You" = the singular reader. Hold that across all 10 sections.

**DeskWolf-specific overrides (Homepage):**
- `deskwolf.ai` Primary Offer = $997/mo AI Receptionist. Other Offers Index = 12 industry pages. Skool $97 surfaces only contextually downstream (lead magnet → email sequence → tripwire) — not on the homepage itself.
- `sites.deskwolf.ai` Primary Offer = $997 build tier; Other Offers = 3 tiers + maintenance recurring.
- Lead Magnet form copy: imperative-verb buttons ("Send me the playbook"). Never "Submit", "Get Started", or "Subscribe."
- Newsletter section names cadence and value: "Weekly AI playbooks for local business owners" — not "stay in touch."
- Founder bios on Meet the Team use Vic's actual voice. Run them past the partnership-oriented voice attribute gate before shipping.
- Public copy never names ClickUp, Resend, or "the CRM." End users don't need to know the backend.

### LANDING PAGE FUNNEL (Single-Offer Sales Pattern)

The landing page sells one offer to one audience with one CTA. Escalating commitment, single CTA destination, loss-aversion language permitted, decision paralysis forbidden. Visitor leaves with one of two things: a purchase, or a tagged exit (the Tripwire downsell).

```
[1. HERO] — Template #1 + Big Domino headline + Value Equation subhead
   Sub-headline: small all-caps, keyword + audience call-out
   Headline: specific desired outcome (Big Domino installed here)
   Body: 2-3 sentences naming features → benefits → outcome
   Primary CTA: action verb + reward ("Book my 15-min call")
   Same gold money-moment treatment the frontend skill enforces

[2. VSL / WEBINAR] — Template #9 (Perfect Webinar 3-Secrets)
   Big Promise → Authority Stack → Future Pace
   Secret 1 (Vehicle belief) → Secret 2 (Internal belief) → Secret 3 (External belief)
   Stack & Close with explicit price reveal
   Length: 18-30 minutes for a $1K-$5K offer; 8-12 minutes for a tripwire tier
   Captions baked in — never assume audio plays

[3. SOCIAL PROOF LOGOS] — Credibility stack copy
   Caption above logos: "Trusted by [N] [audience type]" with concrete number
   Never "Trusted by industry leaders" or "Featured in" without a real list
   Logos themselves carry no copy

[4. BENEFITS / TRANSFORMATION] — Hero's Journey + power-up framing
   Open with: "Are you a [specific person] looking for [specific transformation]?"
   3-6 benefit blocks. Each = transformation headline ("From X to Y") + 1-sentence body
   Power-up framing: each feature is a "tool" or "shortcut" the user collects on the journey
   End the section with the post-transformation outcome stated as already-achieved

[5. CASE STUDIES] — Result-Reframe + specificity rule
   1-3 case study blocks
   Each: client name + situation-before + what they tried that didn't work + new opportunity (your offer) + numeric result + quote
   Numbers are concrete: "added $4,200/month in 60 days" not "boosted revenue"
   No generic "Our clients love us" — every block has a name attached

[6. FEATURE BREAKDOWN — DO THE MATH] — Value Equation expansion
   List every component with attached value
   Format: Component name — what it does for them — value $X
   Total cumulates to anchor the upcoming price reveal
   Goal: reader mentally tallies and says "this offer is super valuable"

[7. MASTER MOCKUP — caption copy]
   Caption uses concrete-noun rule
   "Your AI receptionist handling a 2-AM emergency call for the dental practice across the street" → right
   "Powerful AI that works hard for your business" → wrong
   Caption pulls double duty as alt text

[8. PRICING & PRIMARY CTA] — Template #2 + Template #3 (Anchor)
   Show the cumulative Feature Breakdown total first
   Reveal price (anchored against the total)
   Single tier — no 3-tier comparison grid
   Guarantee badge directly below price
   Gold CTA — same wording as Hero CTA verbatim

[9. CUSTOMER TESTIMONIALS] — Result-Reframe variant
   Different testimonials than section 5's Case Studies
   Format: name + photo + role + numeric result + quote
   3-5 minimum. Specificity gates approval.

[10. BONUSES & TOP FEATURES] — Template #13 (Bonus Stack)
   1-4 bonus blocks
   Each: bonus name (M.A.G.I.C. flavored) + benefit + value
   Cumulative bonus value reinforces the price anchor from section 8
   "When you order today, you also get..." framing

[11. TRIPWIRE / SCARCITY] — Money Models downsell OR Guarantee (Templates #11, #12)
   Pick ONE: low-priced downsell OR honest scarcity OR guarantee section
   Never stack all three — pick one and own it
   On DeskWolf properties, Tripwire is the Skool $97 community downsell
   Copy template:
     "Not ready for [primary offer]?
      Start with the [community name] for $97/mo.
      Same frameworks, do-it-yourself pace."
   Never fake countdown timers. Never fake "only 3 spots left."

[12. FAQ — OBJECTION HANDLING] — False Beliefs framework
   5-10 questions
   Each Q handles a specific objection that isn't already addressed
   Map each Q to one of the 3 false belief categories (Vehicle, Internal, External)
   Answer in the founder's voice, not corporate prose
   No "Great question!" preamble

[13. FINAL CTA] — Echoes Hero CTA verbatim
   Same button text, same destination as section 1
   One sentence above the button restating urgency or guarantee
   Never new offer language at this point — repetition is the rule

[14. FOOTER] — Utility, no copy framework
```

**Voice through-line (Landing):**
- Single CTA destination — every CTA on the page goes to the same place. No "Learn more" buttons going elsewhere.
- Escalating commitment — Hero CTA is the soft ask, Final CTA is the hard ask, identical text but the surrounding copy ramps urgency.
- Loss-aversion language permitted — "What you'll miss without [offer]" framing is fair game once Benefits and Case Studies have established the dream outcome.
- Decision paralysis forbidden — never present multiple options. If the offer has tiers, the page sells one tier. Pricing comparison lives at `/pricing`, not in this funnel.
- Big Domino installed in Hero, reinforced in VSL Secret 1, restated in FAQ when relevant. Repetition is the rule on landing pages — not on homepages.
- Antithetical "not X, but Y" construction is banned — see `copywriting_guidelines.md`. State the new vehicle directly as the positive claim.

**DeskWolf-specific overrides (Landing):**
- Tripwire = Skool $97 community downsell. Hard-coded copy template above. Never fake scarcity.
- Industry pages (`/industries/<slug>`): VSL script uses the industry's specific call-recap as the Authority Stack proof element ("Listen to a real dental front desk handle a 2-AM emergency call").
- Premium properties (`consult.deskwolf.ai`): voice gate flips — first person plural absent, founder name carries authority. Vic is named, not "we."
- Final CTA echoes Hero CTA verbatim. Across the entire DeskWolf funnel network this is a hard rule.
- `unmidify.com/index-launch` follows the full 14-section funnel. Public `/` runs a stripped 2-section variant (Hero + email capture + Footer).
- No competitor names in any section. See Competitor Handling in `copywriting_guidelines.md`.
